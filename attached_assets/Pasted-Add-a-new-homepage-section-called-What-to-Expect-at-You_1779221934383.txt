Add a new homepage section called “What to Expect at Your First Visit,” but do NOT design it as a grid of cards. The site already has several card-based sections, so this section should feel more editorial, open, and premium.

Place it after the services section and before the Meet Dr. Nathan Smith section.

Design direction:
- Use a clean two-column layout on desktop.
- Left side: section heading, short intro paragraph, and a small CTA button.
- Right side: a vertical numbered timeline with 4 steps.
- Do not put each step inside a full card.
- Instead, use large step numbers, thin divider lines, subtle icons if appropriate, and clean text blocks.
- Use plenty of whitespace so it feels calm and professional.
- On mobile, stack the heading first, then the vertical timeline.

Section heading:
What to Expect at Your First Visit

Intro copy:
Your first visit is designed to help us understand your needs, answer your questions, and create a care plan that makes sense for you.

Timeline steps:

01 — Consultation
We’ll talk through your symptoms, health history, and goals so Dr. Smith can better understand what brought you in.

02 — Examination
Dr. Smith will evaluate movement, posture, and areas of discomfort to get a clearer picture of what may be contributing to the issue.

03 — Care Plan
You’ll receive a personalized care plan based on your needs, comfort level, and long-term wellness goals.

04 — Follow-Up Care
We’ll track your progress over time and adjust care as needed to support better movement, comfort, and function.

Also add a small, simple CTA near the intro:
Button text: Request Appointment

Style:
- Cream or off-white background
- Forest green headings
- Muted sage accents for numbers or divider lines
- Elegant serif heading
- Clean readable body text
- No heavy shadows
- No boxed card grid
- Keep it premium, calm, and healthcare/wellness focused

Important:
Avoid making medical guarantees. Keep the language general and reassuring.
